## Overview

This training plan ensures compliance with OSHA regulations and Missouri state requirements for restaurant employees. All training must be completed before employees begin work and updated regularly to maintain compliance.

**Regulatory Bodies:**
- Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA)
- Missouri Department of Labor and Industrial Relations
- Missouri Department of Health and Senior Services
- Local County Health Departments

## General Workplace Safety Training

### 1. OSHA 10-Hour General Industry Training

**Required for:** All entry-level and new employees  
**Duration:** 10 hours  
**Frequency:** Initial training + refresher every 3-5 years

**Topics Covered:**
- Introduction to OSHA and employee rights
- Walking and working surfaces
- Slip, trip, and fall prevention
- Exit routes, emergency action plans, and fire prevention
- Electrical safety
- Hazard communication (GHS/SDS)
- Personal protective equipment (PPE)
- Materials handling and storage
- Machine guarding
- Ergonomics and repetitive motion injuries

**Training Provider Options:**
- Missouri Enterprise (on-site or online)
- OSHA-authorized trainers
- Online OSHA training platforms (OSHA-approved)

### 2. Slip, Trip, and Fall Prevention

**Required for:** All employees  
**Duration:** 30-60 minutes  
**Frequency:** Annual

**Key Topics:**
- Proper cleaning procedures for spills
- Use of wet floor signs
- Proper footwear requirements
- Identifying hazards (wet floors, cords, uneven surfaces)
- Safe carrying techniques
- Proper lighting in work areas
- Stairs and ladder safety

### 3. Fire Safety and Emergency Procedures

**Required for:** All employees  
**Duration:** 1-2 hours  
**Frequency:** Annual + fire drills quarterly

**Key Topics:**
- Location and use of fire extinguishers
- Types of fire extinguishers (Class A, B, C, K)
- Emergency evacuation procedures
- Assembly point locations
- Emergency contact numbers
- Role of fire wardens
- Kitchen hood suppression systems
- Emergency exit locations and routes

### 4. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

**Required for:** All employees handling chemicals, hot surfaces, or sharp objects  
**Duration:** 30-45 minutes  
**Frequency:** Initial + when new PPE introduced

**Key Topics:**
- Cut-resistant gloves for food prep
- Heat-resistant gloves for cooking
- Non-slip footwear
- Aprons and protective clothing
- Eye protection (when handling chemicals)
- Proper use, maintenance, and replacement of PPE

### 5. Electrical Safety

**Required for:** All employees  
**Duration:** 30 minutes  
**Frequency:** Annual

**Key Topics:**
- Identifying electrical hazards
- Proper use of kitchen equipment
- Water and electricity dangers
- Extension cord safety
- Reporting damaged equipment
- Lockout/tagout procedures for maintenance

### 6. Hazard Communication (HazCom)

**Required for:** All employees who handle chemicals  
**Duration:** 1-2 hours  
**Frequency:** Initial + annual refresher

**Key Topics:**
- Safety Data Sheets (SDS) location and interpretation
- Chemical labeling requirements (GHS)
- Pictograms and hazard warnings
- Common restaurant chemicals (cleaning products, sanitizers)
- Proper storage and handling
- Emergency response to chemical exposure

## Food Safety Training

### 1. Food Handler Certification (ServSafe®)

**Required for:** All food handling employees  
**Duration:** 2-4 hours  
**Frequency:** Every 3-5 years (certification renewal)

**Key Topics:**
- Foodborne illness prevention
- Basic food safety practices
- Personal hygiene requirements
- Cross-contamination prevention
- Time and temperature control
- Proper cleaning and sanitation
- Allergen awareness
- Proper food storage

**Training Provider:**
- Missouri Restaurant Association (ServSafe® programs)
- Local health department approved programs
- Online ServSafe® certification

**Note:** While Missouri doesn't mandate certification statewide, having a certified food protection manager demonstrates knowledge and is highly recommended.

### 2. Personal Hygiene Standards

**Required for:** All food handling employees  
**Duration:** 30 minutes  
**Frequency:** Initial + annual refresher

**Key Topics:**
- Proper handwashing (when and how)
- Use of gloves
- Hair restraints
- Jewelry restrictions
- Illness reporting requirements
- Clean uniforms/clothing
- Nail care and restrictions
- Eating, drinking, and smoking policies

### 3. Cross-Contamination Prevention

**Required for:** All food handling employees  
**Duration:** 45 minutes  
**Frequency:** Initial + semi-annual refresher

**Key Topics:**
- Raw vs. cooked food separation
- Color-coded cutting boards and utensils
- Proper food storage order in refrigerators
- Preventing allergen cross-contact
- Common food allergens (Big 8)
- Cleaning and sanitizing between tasks
- Proper use of separate equipment

### 4. Time and Temperature Control

**Required for:** All kitchen staff  
**Duration:** 1 hour  
**Frequency:** Initial + annual refresher

**Key Topics:**
- Temperature danger zone (41°F - 135°F)
- Proper cooking temperatures for different foods
- Cooling procedures (two-stage cooling)
- Reheating requirements
- Cold holding vs. hot holding
- Thermometer use and calibration
- Date marking and FIFO (First In, First Out)

### 5. Cleaning and Sanitation

**Required for:** All employees  
**Duration:** 1 hour  
**Frequency:** Initial + annual refresher

**Key Topics:**
- Three-compartment sink procedures
- Dishwasher operation and temperature requirements
- Sanitizer concentration testing
- Cleaning vs. sanitizing
- Proper cleaning schedules
- Cleaning chemical safety
- Equipment breakdown cleaning

## Additional Required Training

### 1. Respiratory Protection Program

**Required for:** Employees exposed to airborne contaminants  
**Duration:** 1-2 hours  
**Frequency:** Annual + when changes occur

**Key Topics:**
- When respiratory protection is needed
- Types of respirators
- Fit testing procedures
- Medical evaluation requirements
- Cleaning and maintenance
- Cartridge replacement schedules

**Note:** This may apply if employees handle certain chemicals or work in confined spaces with poor ventilation.

**Resource:** Missouri Department of Labor sample written program

### 2. Confined Space Training

**Required for:** Employees who enter confined spaces  
**Duration:** 2-3 hours  
**Frequency:** Annual

**Key Topics:**
- Identifying permit-required confined spaces
- Entry procedures and permits
- Atmospheric testing
- Ventilation requirements
- Emergency rescue procedures
- Role of attendants and entry supervisors

**Examples in Restaurants:**
- Walk-in freezers/coolers (some configurations)
- Grease traps
- Storage tanks
- Crawl spaces under equipment

**Resource:** Missouri Department of Labor confined spaces program resources

### 3. Bloodborne Pathogens

**Required for:** Employees who may encounter blood/bodily fluids  
**Duration:** 1 hour  
**Frequency:** Annual

**Key Topics:**
- Universal precautions
- Proper cleanup procedures
- Use of PPE (gloves)
- Exposure incident reporting
- Hepatitis B vaccination information
- Proper disposal of contaminated materials

### 4. Sexual Harassment Prevention

**Required for:** All employees (recommended best practice)  
**Duration:** 1-2 hours  
**Frequency:** Initial + every 2 years

**Key Topics:**
- Defining harassment and discrimination
- Company policies and reporting procedures
- Bystander intervention
- Creating a respectful workplace
- Legal protections and rights

### 5. Workplace Violence Prevention

**Required for:** All employees (recommended best practice)  
**Duration:** 1 hour  
**Frequency:** Annual

**Key Topics:**
- Recognizing warning signs
- De-escalation techniques
- Robbery response procedures
- Active shooter preparedness
- Reporting procedures
- Security measures

## Training Evaluation and Continuous Improvement

### Assessment Methods

1. **Written Tests:** Minimum 80% passing score
2. **Practical Demonstrations:** Hands-on skill verification
3. **Observation Checklists:** Supervisor evaluations
4. **Safety Audits:** Regular workplace inspections

### Performance Indicators

- Reduction in workplace injuries
- Fewer health code violations
- Improved inspection scores
- Lower workers' compensation claims
- Increased employee confidence

### Continuous Improvement

**Monthly:**
- Review incident reports
- Identify training gaps
- Update procedures as needed

**Quarterly:**
- Safety committee meetings
- Review training effectiveness
- Employee feedback surveys

**Annually:**
- Comprehensive training program review
- Update materials for regulatory changes
- Review and update emergency plans
