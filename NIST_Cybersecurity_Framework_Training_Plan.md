## Overview

This comprehensive training plan is designed to help organizations implement the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0, released in 2024. The framework provides a structured approach to managing cybersecurity risks across all organization types and sizes.

**Framework Version:** NIST CSF 2.0  
**Last Updated:** February 2024  
**Target Audience:** All organizational roles from executives to technical staff

### Why NIST CSF 2.0?

The NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 represents a significant evolution:
- Expanded scope beyond critical infrastructure to all organizations
- Added sixth core function: **Govern**
- Enhanced supply chain risk management
- Improved integration with other frameworks
- Greater emphasis on cybersecurity governance
- Updated to address modern threats and technologies

---

## Framework Introduction

### The Six Core Functions

The NIST CSF 2.0 is built around six core functions that provide a high-level strategic view of an organization's management of cybersecurity risk:

1. **GOVERN (GV)** - *NEW in CSF 2.0*
   - Establishing and monitoring cybersecurity risk management strategy
   - Expectations, policies, and procedures
   - Organizational context, mission, and stakeholder expectations

2. **IDENTIFY (ID)**
   - Understanding cybersecurity risks to systems, people, assets, data, and capabilities
   - Asset management and risk assessment
   - Business environment understanding

3. **PROTECT (PR)**
   - Implementing safeguards to ensure delivery of critical services
   - Access control, data security, and protective technology
   - Training and awareness programs

4. **DETECT (DE)**
   - Identifying the occurrence of cybersecurity events
   - Continuous monitoring and detection processes
   - Anomaly detection and event analysis

5. **RESPOND (RS)**
   - Taking action regarding detected cybersecurity incidents
   - Response planning and communications
   - Analysis and mitigation activities

6. **RECOVER (RC)**
   - Maintaining resilience and restoring capabilities
   - Recovery planning and improvements
   - Lessons learned and adaptation

## Assessment and Certification

### Knowledge Assessments

**Module Quizzes:**
- 10-15 questions per module
- 80% passing score required
- Unlimited attempts allowed
- Immediate feedback provided

**Practical Exercises:**
- Hands-on labs for technical modules
- Scenario-based activities
- Group exercises and workshops
- Real-world application projects

**Final Certification Exam:**
- Comprehensive exam covering all functions
- 100 questions, multiple choice
- 85% passing score required
- 3-hour time limit
- Valid for 3 years

### Certification Levels

**NIST CSF Awareness Certificate**
- Completion of general awareness training
- Basic understanding of framework
- Valid for 2 years

**NIST CSF Practitioner Certificate**
- Completion of role-specific training path
- Passing score on relevant module assessments
- Demonstrates implementation capability
- Valid for 3 years

**NIST CSF Professional Certificate**
- Completion of all modules
- Passing score on comprehensive exam
- Documented implementation experience
- Valid for 3 years

**NIST CSF Master Certificate**
- Professional certification + 2 years experience
- Leadership in framework implementation
- Contribution to organizational security posture
- Valid for 3 years

### Continuing Education

**Annual Requirements:**
- 12 CPE (Continuing Professional Education) hours
- Attend industry conferences or webinars
- Complete advanced training modules
- Participate in security community activities

## Glossary of Terms

**Asset:** Data, personnel, devices, systems, and facilities that enable the organization to achieve business purposes.

**Capability:** A combination of mutually reinforcing cybersecurity controls implemented by technical, physical, and procedural means.

**Category:** Subdivision of a Function into groups of cybersecurity outcomes closely tied to programmatic needs and particular activities.

**Control:** A safeguard or countermeasure prescribed for an information system or an organization.

**Framework Core:** A set of cybersecurity activities, desired outcomes, and applicable references common across critical infrastructure sectors.

**Framework Profile:** A customized selection of Functions, Categories, and Subcategories from the Framework Core based on business needs.

**Governance:** The system by which an organization makes and implements decisions in pursuit of its objectives.

**Implementation Tier:** Characterization of an organization's approach to cybersecurity risk management.

**Profile:** The alignment of Functions, Categories, and Subcategories with business requirements, risk tolerance, and resources.

**Subcategory:** Further division of Categories into specific outcomes of technical and/or management activities.
